TITLE: Term Insurance - Pure Protection

CATEGORY: Insurance

SUMMARY: Term insurance provides pure life coverage at lowest cost. It pays death benefit if insured dies during policy term, offering financial security for dependents.

KEY POINTS:
- Pure protection, no investment component
- Lowest premium among insurance products
- Coverage: Rs. 25,00,000 to Rs. 1,00,00,000+
- Term: 10 to 40 years
- No cash value or maturity benefit
- Affordable for all income levels

PREMIUM FACTORS:
- Age: Younger = lower premium
- Health: Good health = lower premium
- Coverage amount: Higher = higher premium
- Term length: Longer = higher premium
- Occupation: Risky job = higher premium
- Lifestyle: Smoking = higher premium

COVERAGE TYPES:
- Level term: Fixed coverage throughout
- Increasing term: Coverage increases annually
- Decreasing term: Coverage decreases annually
- Convertible term: Can convert to permanent

BEST SUITED FOR:
- Young professionals
- Breadwinners with dependents
- Loan borrowers
- Business owners
- Anyone needing affordable protection
- Family financial security

RISK LEVEL: Low

IMPORTANT GUIDELINES:
- Buy term insurance early
- Coverage: 10-15x annual income
- Maintain good health
- Disclose all health information
- Review coverage every 5 years
- Nominate beneficiary

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Buying too late in life
- Insufficient coverage amount
- Not disclosing health conditions
- Ignoring premium increases
- Not reviewing coverage
- Lapsing policy

SOURCE TYPE: Financial Best Practice

COVERAGE CALCULATION:
Annual income: Rs. 10,00,000
Recommended coverage: Rs. 1,00,00,000 (10x income)
Term: 30 years
Estimated premium: Rs. 300-500 monthly

CLAIM PROCESS:
Beneficiary submits claim form with death certificate. Insurance company verifies and processes claim. Amount credited within 30 days. No tax on death benefit.

COMPARISON:
Term insurance: Rs. 300-500/month, Rs. 1,00,00,000 coverage
Endowment policy: Rs. 5,000-10,000/month, Rs. 10,00,000 coverage
Term insurance provides better coverage at lower cost.
