TITLE: Salary Buffer and Cash Flow Management

CATEGORY: Emergency Fund

SUMMARY: Salary buffer is advance savings from monthly income to handle unexpected expenses. It maintains financial stability and prevents emergency debt.

KEY POINTS:
- Save 10-20% of monthly salary
- Build buffer before investing
- Separate from emergency fund
- Covers 1-2 months expenses
- Prevents credit card debt
- Improves financial flexibility

BUFFER BUILDING:
- Month 1: Save 10% of salary
- Month 2-3: Save 15% of salary
- Month 4-6: Save 20% of salary
- After 6 months: Redirect to investments

SALARY BREAKDOWN:
Monthly salary: Rs. 50,000
Expenses: Rs. 40,000
Buffer savings: Rs. 5,000 (10%)
Remaining: Rs. 5,000 (for investments)

BEST SUITED FOR:
- Salaried employees
- Irregular income earners
- Freelancers
- Business owners
- Anyone with variable expenses
- Young professionals

RISK LEVEL: Low

IMPORTANT GUIDELINES:
- Automate buffer savings
- Keep in accessible account
- Earn interest on balance
- Don't use for non-emergencies
- Replenish after withdrawal
- Review monthly

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Not building buffer
- Using buffer for wants
- Keeping in low-interest account
- Not automating savings
- Mixing with emergency fund
- Ignoring buffer depletion

SOURCE TYPE: Financial Best Practice

CASH FLOW MANAGEMENT:
Income: Rs. 50,000
Fixed expenses: Rs. 30,000
Variable expenses: Rs. 10,000
Buffer savings: Rs. 5,000
Investments: Rs. 5,000

AUTOMATION STRATEGY:
Set up automatic transfer on salary date. Transfer 10-20% to buffer account. Reduces temptation to spend. Builds discipline. Ensures consistent savings.

BUFFER USAGE:
Use only for genuine emergencies. Replenish immediately after use. Maintain minimum 1-month buffer. Increase buffer with income growth.
