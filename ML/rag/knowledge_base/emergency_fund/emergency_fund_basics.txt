TITLE: Emergency Fund Basics

CATEGORY: Emergency Fund

SUMMARY: Emergency fund is liquid savings for unexpected expenses. It prevents debt accumulation during financial emergencies and provides peace of mind.

KEY POINTS:
- Target: 6 months of living expenses
- Minimum: 3 months of expenses
- Ideal: 12 months for self-employed
- Liquid and easily accessible
- Separate from regular savings
- Earns interest in savings account

EMERGENCY FUND SIZE:
- Monthly expenses: Rs. 50,000
- 3-month fund: Rs. 1,50,000
- 6-month fund: Rs. 3,00,000
- 12-month fund: Rs. 6,00,000

WHAT COUNTS AS EMERGENCY:
- Job loss or income reduction
- Medical emergency
- Home or vehicle repair
- Family emergency
- Unexpected expense
- Business disruption

BEST SUITED FOR:
- All individuals
- Salaried employees
- Self-employed professionals
- Business owners
- Families with dependents
- Anyone with financial obligations

RISK LEVEL: Low

IMPORTANT GUIDELINES:
- Build emergency fund first
- Keep in liquid savings account
- Earn interest on balance
- Don't invest in risky assets
- Replenish after withdrawal
- Review annually

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Not building emergency fund
- Insufficient fund size
- Investing in illiquid assets
- Using for non-emergencies
- Not replenishing after use
- Keeping in low-interest account

SOURCE TYPE: Financial Best Practice

BUILDING STRATEGY:
Month 1-3: Save Rs. 10,000 monthly = Rs. 30,000
Month 4-6: Save Rs. 15,000 monthly = Rs. 90,000
Month 7-12: Save Rs. 20,000 monthly = Rs. 1,20,000
Total 12 months: Rs. 2,40,000 (approximately)

ACCOUNT SELECTION:
Use savings account for emergency fund. Earn 3-4% interest. Instant withdrawal facility. No lock-in period. FDIC protection up to Rs. 5,00,000.

EMERGENCY FUND PRIORITY:
Build emergency fund before investing. Prevents debt during emergencies. Provides financial stability. Enables risk-taking in investments.
