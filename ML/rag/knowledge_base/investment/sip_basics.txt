TITLE: Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) Basics

CATEGORY: Investment

SUMMARY: SIP is a disciplined investment method where fixed amounts are invested regularly in mutual funds. It reduces timing risk through rupee cost averaging and builds wealth systematically over time.

KEY POINTS:
- Invest fixed amount monthly (Rs. 500 to Rs. 1,00,000+)
- Automatic deduction from bank account
- Works in all market conditions
- Reduces impact of market volatility
- Builds long-term wealth through compounding
- Flexible tenure from 6 months to 30+ years

BEST SUITED FOR:
- Salaried employees with regular income
- Beginners in investing
- Long-term wealth builders
- Risk-averse investors
- Students starting early

RISK LEVEL: Moderate (depends on fund type)

IMPORTANT GUIDELINES:
- Start SIP early to maximize compounding
- Maintain SIP for minimum 5-10 years
- Choose funds aligned with risk tolerance
- Review portfolio annually
- Increase SIP amount with income growth
- Do not stop SIP during market downturns

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Stopping SIP during market corrections
- Choosing wrong fund category
- Not reviewing fund performance
- Investing without clear goal
- Frequent switching between funds
- Ignoring expense ratios

SOURCE TYPE: Financial Best Practice

RUPEE COST AVERAGING BENEFIT:
When market is high, fewer units purchased. When market is low, more units purchased. This averages out cost per unit over time, reducing timing risk compared to lump sum investment.

CALCULATION EXAMPLE:
Monthly SIP: Rs. 5,000
Duration: 20 years
Expected return: 12% annually
Estimated corpus: Rs. 30,00,000 (approximately)

SIP ADVANTAGES:
Disciplined investing, reduced emotional decisions, automatic investment, flexibility to increase/decrease, tax-efficient options available, suitable for all income levels.
