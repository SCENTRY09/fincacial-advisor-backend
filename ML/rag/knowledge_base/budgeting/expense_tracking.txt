TITLE: Expense Tracking and Analysis

CATEGORY: Budgeting

SUMMARY: Expense tracking records all spending to identify patterns and opportunities for savings. It provides visibility into financial habits and enables better decision-making.

KEY POINTS:
- Record every expense
- Categorize spending
- Identify spending patterns
- Find savings opportunities
- Monitor budget compliance
- Adjust spending habits

TRACKING METHODS:
- Manual notebook
- Spreadsheet
- Mobile app
- Bank statements
- Credit card statements
- Combination approach

EXPENSE CATEGORIES:
- Housing: Rent, maintenance, utilities
- Food: Groceries, dining, delivery
- Transport: Fuel, public transport, parking
- Insurance: Health, life, vehicle
- Entertainment: Movies, games, hobbies
- Shopping: Clothes, accessories, gifts
- Utilities: Phone, internet, electricity
- Miscellaneous: Other expenses

BEST SUITED FOR:
- All individuals
- Budget-conscious people
- Goal-oriented savers
- Debt repayers
- Anyone wanting financial control
- Families

RISK LEVEL: Low

IMPORTANT GUIDELINES:
- Track daily expenses
- Be honest about spending
- Categorize correctly
- Review weekly
- Analyze monthly
- Adjust as needed

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Not tracking small expenses
- Forgetting cash expenses
- Incorrect categorization
- Not reviewing regularly
- Ignoring patterns
- Not taking action

SOURCE TYPE: Financial Best Practice

TRACKING PROCESS:
1. Record expense immediately
2. Note amount and category
3. Keep receipts
4. Update daily
5. Review weekly
6. Analyze monthly

ANALYSIS QUESTIONS:
- Where is most money spent?
- Which categories exceed budget?
- What are discretionary expenses?
- Where can I reduce spending?
- What are spending patterns?
- How can I save more?

SAVINGS OPPORTUNITIES:
Reduce dining out by 50%: Save Rs. 5,000/month
Cancel unused subscriptions: Save Rs. 1,000/month
Reduce shopping: Save Rs. 3,000/month
Total potential savings: Rs. 9,000/month
