TITLE: Budgeting Basics

CATEGORY: Budgeting

SUMMARY: Budgeting is planning income and expenses to achieve financial goals. It provides control over spending and enables systematic wealth building.

KEY POINTS:
- Track income and expenses
- Allocate funds to categories
- Control discretionary spending
- Achieve financial goals
- Identify spending patterns
- Build financial discipline

BUDGET CATEGORIES:
- Fixed expenses: Rent, insurance, EMI
- Variable expenses: Food, utilities, transport
- Discretionary: Entertainment, dining, shopping
- Savings: Emergency fund, investments
- Debt repayment: Loan EMI, credit card

50-30-20 RULE:
- 50% for needs (rent, food, utilities)
- 30% for wants (entertainment, dining)
- 20% for savings and debt repayment

BEST SUITED FOR:
- All individuals
- Salaried employees
- Self-employed professionals
- Business owners
- Families
- Goal-oriented individuals

RISK LEVEL: Low

IMPORTANT GUIDELINES:
- Create realistic budget
- Track actual spending
- Review monthly
- Adjust as needed
- Automate savings
- Maintain discipline

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Unrealistic budget
- Not tracking spending
- Ignoring small expenses
- Not reviewing regularly
- Overspending on wants
- Skipping savings

SOURCE TYPE: Financial Best Practice

BUDGET CREATION STEPS:
1. Calculate monthly income
2. List all expenses
3. Categorize expenses
4. Set spending limits
5. Plan savings
6. Track actual spending

MONTHLY BUDGET EXAMPLE:
Income: Rs. 50,000
Rent: Rs. 15,000
Food: Rs. 8,000
Utilities: Rs. 2,000
Transport: Rs. 3,000
Insurance: Rs. 2,000
Entertainment: Rs. 5,000
Savings: Rs. 10,000
Remaining: Rs. 5,000

BUDGET TOOLS:
Use spreadsheet or budgeting app. Track daily expenses. Review weekly. Adjust monthly. Automate savings. Monitor progress.
