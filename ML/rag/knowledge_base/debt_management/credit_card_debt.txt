TITLE: Credit Card Debt Management

CATEGORY: Debt Management

SUMMARY: Credit card debt carries high interest rates (18-45% annually). Effective management requires understanding charges, paying on time, and strategic repayment to avoid debt spiral.

KEY POINTS:
- Interest rates: 18-45% per annum
- Minimum payment: 5% of outstanding balance
- Grace period: 20-50 days (if full payment made)
- Late payment charges: Rs. 100-500
- Annual fees: Rs. 500-5,000
- Credit limit: Based on income and credit score

CHARGES BREAKDOWN:
- Interest on outstanding balance
- Late payment fees
- Annual membership fees
- Cash advance fees (2-3%)
- Overlimit charges
- GST on all charges

BEST SUITED FOR:
- Salaried employees
- Regular spenders
- Reward seekers
- Emergency backup users
- Disciplined payers

RISK LEVEL: High (if mismanaged)

IMPORTANT GUIDELINES:
- Pay full balance monthly
- Set payment reminders
- Track spending regularly
- Avoid cash advances
- Use rewards wisely
- Monitor credit score

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Paying only minimum amount
- Missing payment deadlines
- Exceeding credit limit
- Using for cash advances
- Ignoring interest charges
- Multiple credit cards without control

SOURCE TYPE: RBI Guidelines

DEBT SPIRAL PREVENTION:
If paying only minimum (5%), Rs. 1,00,000 debt takes 10+ years to clear with Rs. 1,50,000+ total interest. Full payment prevents this.

CREDIT SCORE IMPACT:
Late payments damage credit score for 7 years. Affects future loan approvals and interest rates. Maintain 100% on-time payment record.

EMERGENCY STRATEGY:
If unable to pay full amount, contact bank for payment plan. Negotiate lower interest rate. Avoid defaulting.
