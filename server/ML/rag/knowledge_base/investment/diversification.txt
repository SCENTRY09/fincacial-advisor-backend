TITLE: Portfolio Diversification Strategy

CATEGORY: Investment

SUMMARY: Diversification spreads investments across asset classes, sectors, and geographies to reduce risk. It balances portfolio volatility while maintaining growth potential.

KEY POINTS:
- Spread investments across multiple assets
- Reduces impact of single investment failure
- Balances risk and return
- Includes stocks, bonds, gold, real estate
- Varies by age and risk tolerance
- Rebalance annually

DIVERSIFICATION TYPES:
- Asset class: Stocks, bonds, gold, real estate
- Sector: IT, banking, pharma, FMCG, etc.
- Market cap: Large-cap, mid-cap, small-cap
- Geography: Domestic, international
- Time horizon: Short, medium, long-term

SAMPLE ALLOCATIONS:
Conservative (Age 50+): 30% stocks, 50% bonds, 20% gold
Moderate (Age 35-50): 60% stocks, 30% bonds, 10% gold
Aggressive (Age 25-35): 80% stocks, 15% bonds, 5% gold

BEST SUITED FOR:
- All investors
- Risk-averse investors
- Long-term wealth builders
- Retirees
- Goal-based investors

RISK LEVEL: Low to Moderate

IMPORTANT GUIDELINES:
- Diversify based on risk tolerance
- Rebalance portfolio annually
- Avoid over-diversification
- Maintain emergency fund separately
- Review allocation with life changes
- Consider tax implications

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Over-diversification (too many holdings)
- Concentrating in single sector
- Ignoring asset allocation
- Not rebalancing regularly
- Chasing trends
- Neglecting bonds in young age

SOURCE TYPE: Financial Best Practice

CORRELATION CONCEPT:
Choose assets with low correlation. When one asset falls, others may rise, reducing overall portfolio volatility.

REBALANCING BENEFIT:
Selling winners and buying losers maintains target allocation. Forces disciplined buying low and selling high.
