TITLE: Mutual Funds Overview

CATEGORY: Investment

SUMMARY: Mutual funds pool money from investors to purchase diversified portfolios managed by professionals. They offer accessibility, diversification, and professional management for various investment goals.

KEY POINTS:
- Professional fund manager handles investments
- Diversified portfolio reduces risk
- Low minimum investment (Rs. 500+)
- Daily liquidity through NAV-based pricing
- Regulated by SEBI
- Transparent fee structure

FUND TYPES:
- Equity funds: Higher risk, growth-focused
- Debt funds: Lower risk, income-focused
- Balanced funds: Mixed stocks and bonds
- Liquid funds: Very low risk, quick access

BEST SUITED FOR:
- Retail investors
- First-time investors
- Busy professionals
- Long-term wealth builders
- Risk-averse investors

RISK LEVEL: Low to High (varies by fund type)

IMPORTANT GUIDELINES:
- Check fund manager's track record
- Compare expense ratios
- Understand fund objective before investing
- Invest for minimum 3-5 years
- Avoid frequent buying/selling
- Monitor performance quarterly

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Chasing past performance
- Ignoring expense ratios
- Panic selling during downturns
- Not diversifying across fund types
- Investing without clear goal
- Frequent fund switching

SOURCE TYPE: SEBI Guidelines

EXPENSE RATIO IMPACT:
Lower expense ratio means more returns reach investors. Compare similar funds and choose lower cost options. Even 0.5% difference compounds significantly over 20 years.

NAV CALCULATION:
NAV = (Total Assets - Total Liabilities) / Number of Units
NAV determines investment amount and redemption value.
