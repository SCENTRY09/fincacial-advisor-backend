TITLE: Emergency Planning and Preparedness

CATEGORY: Emergency Fund

SUMMARY: Emergency planning involves identifying potential risks and preparing financial strategies. It ensures readiness for unexpected situations without financial stress.

KEY POINTS:
- Identify potential emergencies
- Assess financial impact
- Build appropriate reserves
- Maintain insurance coverage
- Create action plan
- Review regularly

EMERGENCY TYPES:
- Job loss: 3-6 months expenses
- Medical emergency: Rs. 2,00,000-5,00,000
- Home repair: Rs. 50,000-2,00,000
- Vehicle breakdown: Rs. 20,000-50,000
- Family emergency: Rs. 1,00,000+
- Business disruption: 6-12 months expenses

FINANCIAL PREPAREDNESS:
- Emergency fund: 6 months expenses
- Health insurance: Rs. 5,00,000+
- Term insurance: 10-15x income
- Disability insurance: 60% income
- Accident insurance: Rs. 2,00,000+

BEST SUITED FOR:
- All individuals
- Families with dependents
- Business owners
- Self-employed professionals
- Anyone with financial obligations
- Risk-conscious individuals

RISK LEVEL: Low

IMPORTANT GUIDELINES:
- Assess personal risks
- Build appropriate reserves
- Maintain insurance coverage
- Create emergency contact list
- Document important information
- Review plan annually

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Ignoring potential risks
- Insufficient reserves
- Inadequate insurance
- No action plan
- Not reviewing regularly
- Panic during emergency

SOURCE TYPE: Financial Best Practice

EMERGENCY RESPONSE PLAN:
1. Assess situation
2. Access emergency fund
3. Contact insurance company
4. Seek professional help
5. Document expenses
6. Replenish fund

DOCUMENTATION CHECKLIST:
- Insurance policy numbers
- Bank account details
- Important contacts
- Medical records
- Property documents
- Investment details

RECOVERY STRATEGY:
After emergency, replenish fund immediately. Adjust budget if needed. Review insurance coverage. Update emergency plan. Learn from experience.
