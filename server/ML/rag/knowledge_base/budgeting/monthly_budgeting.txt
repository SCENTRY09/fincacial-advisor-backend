TITLE: Monthly Budgeting Process

CATEGORY: Budgeting

SUMMARY: Monthly budgeting involves planning income and expenses for the month ahead. Regular review and adjustment ensure budget effectiveness and financial goal achievement.

KEY POINTS:
- Plan before month starts
- Review actual vs planned
- Adjust for next month
- Track spending daily
- Identify variances
- Take corrective action

MONTHLY PROCESS:
Week 1: Plan budget for month
Week 2: Track spending
Week 3: Mid-month review
Week 4: Month-end analysis

BUDGET PLANNING:
1. Calculate expected income
2. List fixed expenses
3. Estimate variable expenses
4. Plan discretionary spending
5. Allocate savings
6. Set spending limits

BEST SUITED FOR:
- Salaried employees
- Self-employed professionals
- Business owners
- Families
- Anyone with monthly income
- Goal-oriented individuals

RISK LEVEL: Low

IMPORTANT GUIDELINES:
- Plan realistically
- Track daily
- Review weekly
- Adjust as needed
- Communicate with family
- Celebrate successes

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Unrealistic planning
- Not tracking daily
- Ignoring variances
- Not adjusting
- Overspending on wants
- Not communicating

SOURCE TYPE: Financial Best Practice

MONTHLY BUDGET TEMPLATE:
Income: Rs. 50,000
Fixed expenses: Rs. 20,000
Variable expenses: Rs. 15,000
Discretionary: Rs. 5,000
Savings: Rs. 10,000
Total: Rs. 50,000

VARIANCE ANALYSIS:
Planned rent: Rs. 15,000
Actual rent: Rs. 15,000
Variance: Rs. 0 (On track)

Planned food: Rs. 8,000
Actual food: Rs. 9,500
Variance: Rs. 1,500 (Over budget)

ADJUSTMENT STRATEGY:
Identify over-budget categories. Find savings opportunities. Reduce discretionary spending. Increase savings. Plan for next month.

REVIEW CHECKLIST:
- Income received as expected?
- Fixed expenses within budget?
- Variable expenses controlled?
- Discretionary spending reasonable?
- Savings target achieved?
- Any unexpected expenses?
