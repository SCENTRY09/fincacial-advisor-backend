TITLE: ELSS - Tax-Saving Mutual Funds

CATEGORY: Tax

SUMMARY: ELSS (Equity Linked Savings Scheme) combines tax benefits with growth potential. It offers Section 80C deduction and 3-year lock-in with equity market exposure.

KEY POINTS:
- Deduction under Section 80C: Up to Rs. 1,50,000
- Lock-in period: 3 years
- Invests in equity stocks
- Growth potential: 12-15% annually
- Low expense ratio: 0.5-1%
- Tax-efficient structure

INVESTMENT FEATURES:
- Minimum investment: Rs. 500
- SIP option available
- Lump sum investment allowed
- Daily NAV-based pricing
- Automatic dividend reinvestment
- Transparent holdings

LOCK-IN PERIOD:
- 3 years from investment date
- Cannot withdraw before 3 years
- After 3 years: Full liquidity
- Partial withdrawal: Not allowed during lock-in
- Forced discipline for long-term investing

TAX BENEFITS:
- Section 80C deduction: Rs. 1,50,000
- Long-term capital gains: 10% tax (above Rs. 1 lakh)
- Dividend: Tax-free in hand
- Effective tax rate: Lower than other investments

BEST SUITED FOR:
- Tax-conscious investors
- Long-term wealth builders
- Young professionals
- Salaried employees
- First-time investors
- Growth seekers

RISK LEVEL: Moderate

IMPORTANT GUIDELINES:
- Invest for minimum 3 years
- Use SIP for regular investing
- Choose funds with good track record
- Monitor performance quarterly
- Avoid frequent switching
- Maintain investment documents

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Investing without understanding lock-in
- Chasing past performance
- Frequent fund switching
- Ignoring expense ratios
- Not diversifying across funds
- Panic selling after 3 years

SOURCE TYPE: Tax Rules

COMPARISON WITH OTHER 80C OPTIONS:
ELSS: Growth + tax benefit, 3-year lock-in
PPF: Safety + tax benefit, 15-year lock-in
NSC: Safety + tax benefit, 5-year lock-in
ELSS best for growth-seeking investors.

CALCULATION EXAMPLE:
Annual investment: Rs. 1,50,000 via SIP
Duration: 5 years
Expected return: 12% annually
Tax saved: Rs. 45,000 (30% tax rate)
Estimated corpus: Rs. 10,00,000 (approximately)

FUND SELECTION:
Compare expense ratios, fund manager track record, and performance consistency. Choose funds with 5-year positive returns.
