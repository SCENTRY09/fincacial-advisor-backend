TITLE: Old vs New Tax Regime Comparison

CATEGORY: Tax

SUMMARY: India offers two tax regimes with different rates and deductions. Choosing the right regime can save significant taxes based on income and investment patterns.

KEY POINTS:
- Old regime: Deductions available, higher rates
- New regime: No deductions, lower rates
- Choice available annually
- Default: New regime from FY 2023-24
- Can switch between regimes
- Different tax brackets

OLD TAX REGIME:
- Deductions under 80C, 80D, 80E, etc.
- Standard deduction: Rs. 50,000
- Home loan interest: Rs. 2,00,000
- Medical insurance: Rs. 25,000-75,000
- Higher tax rates: 5-30%
- Suitable for high deductions

NEW TAX REGIME:
- No deductions available
- No standard deduction
- No home loan interest deduction
- No medical insurance deduction
- Lower tax rates: 5-30%
- Suitable for low deductions

TAX BRACKETS (OLD REGIME):
- Up to Rs. 2,50,000: Nil
- Rs. 2,50,001-5,00,000: 5%
- Rs. 5,00,001-10,00,000: 20%
- Above Rs. 10,00,000: 30%

TAX BRACKETS (NEW REGIME):
- Up to Rs. 3,00,000: Nil
- Rs. 3,00,001-6,00,000: 5%
- Rs. 6,00,001-9,00,000: 10%
- Rs. 9,00,001-12,00,000: 15%
- Rs. 12,00,001-15,00,000: 20%
- Above Rs. 15,00,000: 30%

BEST SUITED FOR:
Old regime: High deductions, home loan, insurance
New regime: Low deductions, simple income
Salaried employees: Compare both
Business owners: Usually old regime

RISK LEVEL: Low

IMPORTANT GUIDELINES:
- Calculate tax under both regimes
- Choose regime with lower tax
- Decision made while filing ITR
- Can change regime annually
- Maintain deduction proofs
- Plan investments accordingly

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Not comparing both regimes
- Assuming new regime always better
- Not maintaining deduction proofs
- Forgetting to claim deductions
- Not reviewing annually
- Missing investment deadlines

SOURCE TYPE: Tax Rules

COMPARISON EXAMPLE:
Income: Rs. 10,00,000
Old regime deductions: Rs. 2,00,000 (80C + 80D)
Old regime tax: Rs. 1,50,000
New regime tax: Rs. 1,75,000
Old regime saves: Rs. 25,000

DECISION FRAMEWORK:
If deductions > Rs. 2,50,000: Choose old regime
If deductions < Rs. 2,50,000: Compare both
If no deductions: Choose new regime
Review annually as situation changes

PLANNING STRATEGY:
Maximize deductions in old regime. Invest in 80C, 80D, 80E options. Compare tax liability annually. Switch regime if beneficial.
