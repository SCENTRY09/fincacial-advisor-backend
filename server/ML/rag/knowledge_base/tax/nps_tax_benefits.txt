TITLE: National Pension System (NPS) Tax Benefits

CATEGORY: Tax

SUMMARY: NPS provides dual tax benefits under Section 80C and 80CCD(1B). It offers retirement corpus building with government co-contribution and flexible investment options.

KEY POINTS:
- Section 80C deduction: Up to Rs. 1,50,000
- Section 80CCD(1B) deduction: Additional Rs. 50,000
- Total deduction: Up to Rs. 2,00,000
- Government co-contribution: 10% of salary
- Flexible investment options
- Portable across India

TAX DEDUCTIONS:
- Employee contribution: Section 80C (Rs. 1,50,000)
- Additional contribution: Section 80CCD(1B) (Rs. 50,000)
- Employer contribution: Section 80CCD(2) (10% of salary)
- Total tax benefit: Up to Rs. 2,00,000 deduction

INVESTMENT OPTIONS:
- Equity: 50-100% allocation
- Debt: 0-50% allocation
- Government securities: 0-50% allocation
- Liquid funds: 0-50% allocation
- Flexible switching allowed

ACCOUNT TYPES:
- Tier 1: Retirement account (locked until 60)
- Tier 2: Savings account (flexible withdrawal)
- Both accounts can be opened simultaneously

BEST SUITED FOR:
- Salaried employees
- Self-employed professionals
- Business owners
- Retirement planners
- Long-term wealth builders
- Tax-conscious investors

RISK LEVEL: Moderate

IMPORTANT GUIDELINES:
- Open account before age 60
- Maintain regular contributions
- Choose appropriate asset allocation
- Monitor performance quarterly
- Review allocation with age
- Maintain investment documents

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Not utilizing full deduction limit
- Ignoring employer co-contribution
- Inappropriate asset allocation
- Frequent switching
- Not reviewing portfolio
- Withdrawing prematurely

SOURCE TYPE: Tax Rules

TAX SAVING CALCULATION:
Employee contribution: Rs. 1,50,000
Additional contribution: Rs. 50,000
Employer co-contribution: Rs. 1,00,000 (10% of Rs. 10,00,000 salary)
Total annual contribution: Rs. 3,00,000
Tax saved (30% rate): Rs. 60,000

RETIREMENT CORPUS EXAMPLE:
Monthly contribution: Rs. 10,000
Duration: 30 years (age 30 to 60)
Expected return: 10% annually
Estimated corpus: Rs. 1,50,00,000 (approximately)

WITHDRAWAL RULES:
At age 60: 60% can be withdrawn as lump sum. 40% must be used for annuity. Annuity provides monthly pension. Flexibility in withdrawal timing.
