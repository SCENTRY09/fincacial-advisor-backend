TITLE: Section 80C Tax Deductions

CATEGORY: Tax

SUMMARY: Section 80C allows deduction up to Rs. 1,50,000 annually for specified investments and expenses. It reduces taxable income and encourages savings and investment.

KEY POINTS:
- Maximum deduction: Rs. 1,50,000 per year
- Applicable to all income earners
- Reduces taxable income
- Lowers tax liability
- Multiple investment options available
- Deduction claimed in income tax return

ELIGIBLE INVESTMENTS:
- Life insurance premiums
- Provident fund contributions
- Public Provident Fund (PPF)
- National Savings Certificate (NSC)
- Fixed deposits (5-year)
- Sukanya Samriddhi Account
- Tuition fees for education
- Home loan principal repayment
- ELSS mutual funds
- National Pension System (NPS)

INVESTMENT LIMITS:
- PPF: Up to Rs. 1,50,000 annually
- NSC: Up to Rs. 1,50,000 annually
- ELSS: Up to Rs. 1,50,000 annually
- Life insurance: Varies by policy
- Home loan principal: Varies by loan
- Tuition fees: Varies by institution

BEST SUITED FOR:
- Salaried employees
- Self-employed professionals
- Business owners
- High-income earners
- Tax-conscious investors
- Long-term savers

RISK LEVEL: Low

IMPORTANT GUIDELINES:
- Invest before March 31 each year
- Maintain investment proofs
- Don't exceed Rs. 1,50,000 limit
- Claim deduction in ITR
- Keep documents for 5 years
- Update PAN details

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Claiming deduction without proof
- Exceeding Rs. 1,50,000 limit
- Claiming same investment twice
- Missing investment deadline
- Not maintaining documentation
- Claiming ineligible expenses

SOURCE TYPE: Tax Rules

PLANNING STRATEGY:
Combine multiple investments to maximize deduction. PPF (Rs. 50,000) + ELSS (Rs. 50,000) + Life Insurance (Rs. 50,000) = Rs. 1,50,000 deduction.

TAX SAVING CALCULATION:
Deduction: Rs. 1,50,000
Tax rate: 30%
Tax saved: Rs. 45,000 annually
Over 20 years: Rs. 9,00,000 saved

DOCUMENTATION REQUIRED:
Investment certificates, receipts, policy documents, bank statements, tuition fee receipts, loan statements, proof of payment.
