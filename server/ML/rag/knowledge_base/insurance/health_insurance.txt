TITLE: Health Insurance Planning

CATEGORY: Insurance

SUMMARY: Health insurance covers medical expenses for hospitalization, treatment, and medicines. It protects against financial burden of unexpected health emergencies.

KEY POINTS:
- Coverage: Rs. 3,00,000 to Rs. 50,00,000+
- Covers hospitalization expenses
- Includes pre and post-hospitalization
- Covers surgeries and treatments
- Deductible under Section 80D
- Cashless treatment at network hospitals

COVERAGE TYPES:
- Individual health insurance
- Family health insurance
- Group health insurance
- Senior citizen health insurance
- Critical illness insurance
- Accident insurance

DEDUCTIBLE BENEFITS:
- Self: Rs. 15,000 annually
- Family: Rs. 25,000 annually
- Senior citizen (60+): Rs. 50,000 annually
- Parents (60+): Rs. 25,000 annually
- Total family: Rs. 75,000 annually

BEST SUITED FOR:
- All individuals
- Families with dependents
- Self-employed professionals
- Senior citizens
- Chronic disease patients
- Anyone with health risks

RISK LEVEL: Low

IMPORTANT GUIDELINES:
- Buy health insurance early
- Maintain continuous coverage
- Disclose pre-existing conditions
- Review coverage annually
- Increase coverage with inflation
- Keep policy documents safe

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Buying insufficient coverage
- Not disclosing health conditions
- Lapsing policy
- Not reviewing coverage
- Ignoring waiting periods
- Delaying claim submission

SOURCE TYPE: Financial Best Practice

COVERAGE CALCULATION:
Annual income: Rs. 10,00,000
Recommended coverage: Rs. 5,00,000-10,00,000
Family size: 4 members
Estimated premium: Rs. 300-500 monthly

CLAIM PROCESS:
Inform insurance company before hospitalization. Provide policy number and documents. Hospital submits claim directly. Cashless treatment at network hospitals. Reimbursement for non-network hospitals.

WAITING PERIODS:
General waiting: 30 days
Pre-existing conditions: 2-4 years
Maternity: 9-12 months
Critical illness: 30-90 days
Plan accordingly for coverage.
