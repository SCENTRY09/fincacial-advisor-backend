TITLE: Debt Consolidation Strategy

CATEGORY: Debt Management

SUMMARY: Debt consolidation combines multiple debts into single loan with lower interest rate. Simplifies management, reduces interest burden, and accelerates debt repayment.

KEY POINTS:
- Combine multiple debts into one
- Lower interest rate than original debts
- Single monthly payment
- Simplified tracking
- Improved cash flow
- Better credit score potential

CONSOLIDATION OPTIONS:
- Personal loan: Unsecured, quick approval
- Home equity loan: Secured, lower rates
- Balance transfer: Credit card to lower rate
- Debt consolidation loan: Specialized product
- Refinancing: Replace existing loans

WHEN TO CONSOLIDATE:
- Multiple high-interest debts
- Struggling with multiple payments
- Interest rates have dropped
- Credit score improved
- Need cash flow relief
- Simplification required

BEST SUITED FOR:
- Multiple debt holders
- High-interest debt carriers
- Busy professionals
- Disciplined borrowers
- Goal-oriented individuals

RISK LEVEL: Moderate

IMPORTANT GUIDELINES:
- Compare interest rates carefully
- Calculate total interest savings
- Avoid extending tenure unnecessarily
- Don't accumulate new debt
- Maintain emergency fund
- Review terms before consolidating

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Consolidating without rate comparison
- Extending tenure to reduce EMI
- Taking new debt after consolidation
- Ignoring hidden charges
- Not reading fine print
- Consolidating without clear plan

SOURCE TYPE: Financial Best Practice

CALCULATION EXAMPLE:
Original debts: Credit card (36%), personal loan (15%), auto loan (10%)
Consolidated into: Personal loan at 12%
Monthly payment: Reduced by 30-40%
Total interest saved: Rs. 1,00,000+ over tenure

CONSOLIDATION BENEFITS:
Single payment easier to manage. Lower interest rate reduces total burden. Improved cash flow for other goals. Better credit score with on-time payments.

CAUTION:
Consolidation doesn't reduce total debt. It restructures it. Avoid accumulating new debt after consolidation.
