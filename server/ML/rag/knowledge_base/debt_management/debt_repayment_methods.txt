TITLE: Debt Repayment Methods

CATEGORY: Debt Management

SUMMARY: Different debt repayment strategies suit different situations. Choosing the right method accelerates debt freedom and minimizes total interest paid.

KEY POINTS:
- Debt snowball: Pay smallest debt first
- Debt avalanche: Pay highest interest first
- Balanced approach: Mix of both methods
- Prepayment reduces total interest
- Refinancing lowers interest rates
- Consolidation simplifies management

DEBT SNOWBALL METHOD:
- List debts by amount (smallest to largest)
- Pay minimum on all debts
- Extra payment to smallest debt
- Once cleared, move to next
- Psychological wins motivate continuation
- Best for multiple small debts

DEBT AVALANCHE METHOD:
- List debts by interest rate (highest to lowest)
- Pay minimum on all debts
- Extra payment to highest interest debt
- Saves maximum interest
- Mathematically optimal
- Best for high-interest debts

BALANCED APPROACH:
- Combine both methods
- Pay high-interest debts aggressively
- Clear small debts for momentum
- Customize based on situation
- Flexible and practical
- Maintains motivation

BEST SUITED FOR:
- Multiple debt holders
- High-interest debt carriers
- Goal-oriented individuals
- Disciplined savers
- Debt-free seekers

RISK LEVEL: Low

IMPORTANT GUIDELINES:
- Choose method matching your psychology
- Maintain emergency fund during repayment
- Avoid new debt while repaying
- Track progress regularly
- Celebrate milestones
- Stay committed to plan

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Switching methods frequently
- Taking new debt while repaying
- Ignoring emergency fund
- Unrealistic repayment timelines
- Not tracking progress
- Giving up too early

SOURCE TYPE: Financial Best Practice

PREPAYMENT STRATEGY:
Allocate bonus, tax refunds, or extra income to debt repayment. Even Rs. 5,000-10,000 extra monthly accelerates debt freedom significantly.

REFINANCING OPTION:
If interest rates drop, refinance at lower rate. Reduces EMI or tenure. Saves substantial interest over loan period.
